CODE OF CONDUCT
Professional Standards and Ethical Guidelines
Our Commitment: The NICEHR Group is committed to conducting business with the highest standards of integrity, professionalism, and ethical behavior. As healthcare IT consultants, we hold ourselves to the same rigorous standards as the healthcare organizations we serve. This Code of Conduct applies to all employees, contractors, and representatives of The NICEHR Group.

1. Core Values

Our business practices are guided by four core values:

Value What It Means
Integrity We are honest and transparent in all our dealings. We do what we say we will do, even when no one is watching.
Excellence We deliver the highest quality work and continuously strive to improve our skills and services.
Respect We treat all individuals with dignity, valuing diverse perspectives and fostering inclusive environments.
Accountability We take responsibility for our actions, acknowledge our mistakes, and learn from them.

2. Professional Conduct

2.1 Workplace Behavior

All employees are expected to:

  • Conduct themselves professionally at all times, whether at Company offices, Client sites, or remote locations
  • Maintain a respectful and collaborative work environment
  • Communicate clearly, honestly, and constructively with colleagues and Clients
  • Be punctual and reliable in meeting commitments and deadlines
  • Dress appropriately for the work environment (business casual or as required by Client)
  • Represent the Company positively in all professional interactions

2.2 Client Relationships

When working with Clients, employees must:

  • Act in the Client's best interest while maintaining Company obligations
  • Provide accurate and honest assessments of project status, risks, and issues
  • Respect Client confidentiality and proprietary information
  • Follow Client policies, procedures, and security requirements
  • Maintain professional boundaries with Client staff
  • Escalate concerns or conflicts through proper channels
  • Never make commitments on behalf of the Company without authorization

2.3 Communication Standards

  • Use professional language in all written and verbal communications
  • Respond to emails and messages within one business day
  • Keep stakeholders informed of project progress and issues
  • Document important decisions and communications
  • Use Company-approved communication channels for work-related matters

3. Ethical Standards

3.1 Honesty and Transparency

  • Never falsify timesheets, expense reports, or other business records
  • Report project status, issues, and risks accurately
  • Acknowledge limitations in knowledge or expertise
  • Do not misrepresent qualifications, certifications, or experience
  • Correct any errors or misstatements promptly

3.2 Conflicts of Interest

Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict with Company or Client interests:

  • Disclose any personal, financial, or family relationships that may create a conflict
  • Do not accept gifts, entertainment, or favors that could influence business decisions (gifts over $50 must be reported)
  • Do not engage in outside employment that competes with the Company
  • Do not use Company resources for personal gain
  • Do not take business opportunities discovered through Company work
  • Recuse yourself from decisions where you have a personal interest

3.3 Financial Integrity

  • Submit accurate and timely timesheets reflecting actual hours worked
  • Submit only legitimate, pre-approved business expenses with proper documentation
  • Never accept or offer bribes, kickbacks, or improper payments
  • Report any suspected financial misconduct immediately
  • Protect Company and Client financial information

4. Confidentiality and Privacy

4.1 Protected Health Information (PHI)

As healthcare IT consultants, we frequently access patient health information. Employees must:

  • Complete all required HIPAA training before accessing PHI
  • Access PHI only when necessary to perform job duties (minimum necessary standard)
  • Never access PHI for personal curiosity or unauthorized purposes
  • Never share PHI with unauthorized individuals, including family and friends
  • Never photograph, copy, or remove PHI from Client systems without authorization
  • Report any actual or suspected PHI breaches immediately
  • Use only encrypted devices and secure connections when handling PHI

4.2 Confidential Business Information

  • Protect Company trade secrets, client lists, pricing, and business strategies
  • Protect Client confidential information, including system configurations and workflows
  • Do not discuss confidential matters in public places
  • Use secure methods for transmitting confidential information
  • Return all confidential materials upon termination
  • Obligations continue after employment ends

4.3 Social Media and Public Statements

  • Do not post confidential Company or Client information on social media
  • Do not post photos from Client sites without permission
  • Clearly distinguish personal opinions from Company positions
  • Do not make public statements on behalf of the Company without authorization
  • Report any media inquiries to Company management

5. Workplace Safety and Respect

5.1 Harassment-Free Workplace

The Company prohibits all forms of harassment, including but not limited to:

  • Sexual harassment: Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, or other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature
  • Discriminatory harassment: Offensive conduct based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, or any other protected characteristic
  • Bullying: Repeated, unreasonable actions intended to intimidate, degrade, or humiliate
  • Retaliation: Adverse action against anyone who reports harassment or participates in an investigation

5.2 Non-Discrimination

The Company is an equal opportunity employer and prohibits discrimination in all employment practices. We make employment decisions based solely on qualifications, merit, and business needs.

5.3 Workplace Violence

Violence, threats of violence, and intimidating behavior are strictly prohibited. This includes:

  • Physical assault or threatening physical harm
  • Verbal threats or intimidation
  • Possession of weapons on Company or Client premises
  • Stalking or harassing behavior

5.4 Substance Abuse

  • Working while impaired by alcohol or drugs is prohibited
  • Use of illegal drugs is prohibited at all times
  • Employees must comply with Client drug testing requirements
  • Prescription medications that may impair performance must be disclosed to HR

6. Information Technology and Security

6.1 Acceptable Use

  • Use Company and Client IT resources only for authorized business purposes
  • Do not install unauthorized software on Company or Client systems
  • Do not use personal devices to access Client systems without authorization
  • Report lost or stolen devices immediately
  • Log off or lock workstations when leaving them unattended

6.2 Password and Access Security

  • Use strong, unique passwords for all accounts
  • Never share passwords or access credentials
  • Enable multi-factor authentication where available
  • Report suspected unauthorized access immediately
  • Access only systems and data necessary for your job duties

6.3 Data Protection

  • Encrypt sensitive data in transit and at rest
  • Use only approved cloud services and storage solutions
  • Do not transfer Company or Client data to personal devices or accounts
  • Dispose of sensitive documents and media securely
  • Follow Client data handling and retention policies

7. Legal and Regulatory Compliance

7.1 Healthcare Regulations

Employees must comply with all applicable healthcare laws and regulations, including:

  • HIPAA: Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (privacy and security)
  • HITECH: Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act
  • State Privacy Laws: Applicable state-specific healthcare privacy requirements
  • Fraud and Abuse Laws: Anti-kickback statute, False Claims Act, Stark Law

7.2 Business Regulations

  • Comply with all applicable federal, state, and local laws
  • Maintain required professional licenses and certifications
  • Cooperate with legal and regulatory inquiries
  • Preserve documents and records as required by law and policy

7.3 Intellectual Property

  • Respect copyrights, trademarks, and patents of others
  • Use only properly licensed software
  • Protect Company intellectual property and trade secrets
  • Assign work product to Company as required by employment agreement

8. Reporting Violations

How to Report

If you witness or become aware of any violation of this Code of Conduct, you have an obligation to report it. Reports can be made through any of the following channels:

  • Direct Supervisor: Speak with your immediate supervisor
  • Human Resources: Contact HR at HR@nicehrgroup.com
  • Company Leadership: Contact any member of the leadership team
  • Anonymous Reporting: Submit an anonymous report through the Company's ethics hotline

Non-Retaliation Policy: The Company strictly prohibits retaliation against anyone who reports a concern in good faith or participates in an investigation. Retaliation is itself a violation of this Code and will result in disciplinary action.

9. Violations and Consequences

Disciplinary Action

Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment. The severity of the consequence will depend on:

  • The nature and severity of the violation
  • Whether the violation was intentional or negligent
  • The employee's prior conduct history
  • The impact on the Company, Clients, or other individuals
  • Whether the employee self-reported the violation

Examples of Immediate Termination Offenses:

  • Intentional breach of patient privacy (HIPAA violation)
  • Falsification of timesheets or expense reports
  • Theft or misappropriation of Company or Client property
  • Violence, threats, or weapons in the workplace
  • Working while impaired by drugs or alcohol
  • Sexual harassment or discrimination
  • Disclosure of trade secrets to competitors
