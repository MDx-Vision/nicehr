NON-DISCLOSURE AND CONFIDENTIALITY AGREEMENT
This Non-Disclosure and Confidentiality Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of the date set forth below ("Effective Date") by and between:

The NICEHR Group, LLC, a New York limited liability company with its principal place of business in New York ("Company"), and

___________________________________ ("Employee"), an individual residing at ___________________________________.

Company and Employee are collectively referred to as the "Parties" and individually as a "Party."

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Company is engaged in the business of healthcare IT consulting, including Electronic Health Record (EHR) implementations, Technical Dress Rehearsals (TDR), go-live support, and related services for healthcare organizations;
WHEREAS, Employee will be employed by Company and, in connection with such employment, will have access to Confidential Information (as defined below) belonging to Company, its clients, and affiliates;
WHEREAS, Company's clients include healthcare organizations that maintain Protected Health Information (PHI) subject to the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA), as amended;
NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of Employee's employment, access to Confidential Information, compensation, and other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

ARTICLE 1: DEFINITIONS

1.1 "Confidential Information" means any and all non-public information, in any form or medium, that is disclosed to or obtained by Employee in connection with Employee's employment, including but not limited to:
  1. Client names, contacts, systems, configurations, workflows, and business information;
  2. Protected Health Information (PHI) as defined by HIPAA;
  3. Trade secrets, proprietary methodologies, implementation approaches, and technical processes;
  4. Business strategies, plans, pricing, proposals, and financial information;
  5. Employee and consultant information, compensation data, and personnel records;
  6. Software, code, algorithms, data models, and technical documentation;
  7. Training materials, templates, checklists, and operational procedures;
  8. Vendor relationships, contract terms, and supplier information;
  9. Any information marked as "Confidential," "Proprietary," or similar designation; and
  10. Any information that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure.
1.2 "Protected Health Information" or "PHI" has the meaning set forth in the HIPAA Privacy Rule (45 CFR ยง 160.103) and includes any individually identifiable health information held or transmitted by a covered entity or business associate in any form or medium.
1.3 "Client" means any healthcare organization, hospital, health system, physician practice, or other entity for which Company provides services and at which Employee performs work.

ARTICLE 2: NON-DISCLOSURE OBLIGATIONS

2.1 Duty of Confidentiality. Employee agrees to hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and not to disclose, publish, or otherwise reveal any Confidential Information to any third party during or after Employee's employment, except as authorized in writing by Company or as required for the proper performance of Employee's duties.
2.2 Use Restrictions. Employee shall use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing Employee's job duties and for no other purpose. Employee shall not use Confidential Information for personal benefit, for the benefit of any third party, or to compete with Company.
2.3 Standard of Care. Employee shall protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care that Employee uses to protect Employee's own most sensitive confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care.
2.4 No Unauthorized Copies. Employee shall not make copies, reproductions, or derivatives of Confidential Information except as necessary for the performance of Employee's duties. Employee shall not store Confidential Information on personal devices, personal cloud storage, or personal email accounts.
2.5 Access Controls. Employee shall access Confidential Information only through authorized systems and methods, shall maintain the security of login credentials, and shall not share credentials with any other person.

ARTICLE 3: HIPAA AND PHI COMPLIANCE

3.1 PHI Protection. Employee acknowledges that PHI is subject to heightened protection under federal law. Employee agrees to:
  1. Access only the minimum necessary PHI required for assigned job duties;
  2. Never access PHI for personal reasons, curiosity, or any unauthorized purpose;
  3. Never disclose PHI to unauthorized individuals, including family members, friends, or other employees without a need to know;
  4. Use only secure, Company-approved methods for accessing, transmitting, or storing PHI;
  5. Never take screenshots, photographs, or recordings of PHI;
  6. Never remove PHI from client premises except as authorized;
  7. Log off all systems when not in active use; and
  8. Complete all required HIPAA training prior to accessing any PHI.
3.2 Breach Notification. Employee shall immediately notify Company of any actual or suspected breach of PHI, unauthorized access, loss of devices containing PHI, or any security incident. "Immediately" means within twenty-four (24) hours of discovery or, in the case of an ongoing breach, as soon as reasonably possible.
3.3 HIPAA Penalties. Employee acknowledges that HIPAA violations may result in:
  1. Civil penalties up to $1,500,000 per violation category per calendar year;
  2. Criminal penalties including fines up to $250,000 and imprisonment up to 10 years for willful violations;
  3. Personal liability for Employee, separate from any liability of Company;
  4. Exclusion from participation in federal healthcare programs; and
  5. Immediate termination of employment.

ARTICLE 4: EXCLUSIONS FROM CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

Confidential Information does not include information that:
  1. Was publicly known at the time of disclosure through no fault of Employee;
  2. Becomes publicly known after disclosure through no fault of Employee;
  3. Was rightfully in Employee's possession before disclosure, without any obligation of confidentiality, as evidenced by written records;
  4. Is rightfully obtained by Employee from a third party without breach of any confidentiality obligation; or
  5. Is independently developed by Employee without use of or reference to Confidential Information, as evidenced by written records.
NOTWITHSTANDING THE FOREGOING, PHI IS NEVER EXCLUDED from the protections of this Agreement regardless of how Employee obtained access to it.

ARTICLE 5: WORK PRODUCT AND INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

5.1 Work Product Ownership. All work product, deliverables, documentation, training materials, templates, code, configurations, and other materials created by Employee during employment, whether alone or jointly with others ("Work Product"), are and shall remain the sole property of Company and/or its Clients.
5.2 Assignment. Employee hereby irrevocably assigns to Company all right, title, and interest in and to any Work Product, including all intellectual property rights therein, including patents, copyrights, trade secrets, and trademarks.
5.3 Assistance. Employee agrees to execute any documents and take any actions reasonably requested by Company to evidence, perfect, or protect Company's rights in Work Product.

ARTICLE 6: NON-SOLICITATION

6.1 Non-Solicitation of Employees. During employment and for twelve (12) months following termination, Employee agrees not to, directly or indirectly, recruit, solicit, or induce any employee or consultant of Company to terminate their relationship with Company.
6.2 Non-Solicitation of Clients. During employment and for twelve (12) months following termination, Employee agrees not to solicit or encourage any Client to terminate or diminish its relationship with Company, or to divert any business from Company.
6.3 Clarification. This is a non-solicitation agreement, not a non-compete agreement. Employee remains free to work in the healthcare IT industry, including for competitors, subject only to the non-solicitation restrictions above and the ongoing confidentiality obligations herein.

ARTICLE 7: RETURN OF MATERIALS

Upon termination of employment for any reason, or upon Company's request at any time, Employee shall immediately:
  1. Return all documents, files, equipment, badges, access cards, and materials containing or relating to Confidential Information;
  2. Return all Company and Client property in Employee's possession or control;
  3. Permanently delete all Confidential Information from personal devices, cloud storage, and email accounts;
  4. Provide all passwords, access codes, and encryption keys for any Company or Client systems or files; and
  5. Provide written certification of compliance with this Section upon request.

ARTICLE 8: TERM AND SURVIVAL

8.1 Term. This Agreement is effective as of the Effective Date and continues throughout Employee's employment and thereafter.
8.2 Survival. Employee's obligations regarding Confidential Information shall survive termination of employment and continue: (a) indefinitely with respect to trade secrets and PHI; and (b) for five (5) years with respect to all other Confidential Information. The non-solicitation provisions shall survive for twelve (12) months following termination.

ARTICLE 9: REMEDIES

9.1 Irreparable Harm. Employee acknowledges that unauthorized disclosure of Confidential Information would cause irreparable harm to Company and its Clients for which monetary damages would be inadequate.
9.2 Equitable Relief. In addition to any other remedies available, Company shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief, specific performance, and other equitable remedies without the necessity of proving actual damages or posting bond.
9.3 Attorneys' Fees. In any action to enforce this Agreement, the prevailing party shall be entitled to recover reasonable attorneys' fees, costs, and expenses.

ARTICLE 10: GENERAL PROVISIONS

10.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties regarding confidentiality and supersedes all prior agreements and understandings on this subject.
10.2 Amendment. This Agreement may only be amended in writing signed by both Parties.
10.3 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect, and the invalid provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid and enforceable.
10.4 Waiver. No waiver of any provision shall constitute a waiver of any other provision or of the same provision on another occasion.
10.5 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of New York without regard to conflict of law principles.
10.6 Dispute Resolution. Any dispute arising under this Agreement shall be resolved through binding arbitration in New York, New York, in accordance with the rules of the American Arbitration Association.
10.7 Assignment. Employee may not assign this Agreement. Company may assign this Agreement to any successor, affiliate, or acquirer.
10.8 Counterparts. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original. Electronic and digital signatures shall be valid and binding.
