Form W-4: Employee's Withholding Certificate

Form W-4 tells your employer how much federal income tax to withhold from your paycheck. You must complete this form before your first payroll can be processed.

Download Form W-4: https://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-pdf/fw4.pdf

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Enter Personal Information
  • First name, middle initial, last name
  • Social Security Number
  • Address, city, state, ZIP code
  • Filing status: Single, Married filing jointly, or Head of household
Step 2: Multiple Jobs or Spouse Works
  • Complete only if you have more than one job, or if married filing jointly and your spouse also works
  • Options: Use IRS Tax Withholding Estimator, use Multiple Jobs Worksheet, or check box 2(c)
  • If this is your only job and you're single, skip to Step 3
Step 3: Claim Dependents
  • If total income is $200,000 or less ($400,000 married filing jointly):
  • Multiply qualifying children under 17 by $2,000
  • Multiply other dependents by $500
  • Add together and enter total
Step 4: Other Adjustments (Optional)
  • (a) Other income not from jobs
  • (b) Deductions beyond standard deduction
  • (c) Extra withholding per pay period
Step 5: Sign and Date
  • Sign your name and enter today's date
  • The form is not valid without your signature

Common Scenarios

  • Single, one job, no dependents: Complete Steps 1 and 5 only
  • Married, both work: Complete Steps 1, 2, and 5
  • Single parent with children: Complete Steps 1, 3, and 5

Form I-9: Employment Eligibility Verification

Form I-9 verifies that you are authorized to work in the United States. This is a federal requirement for all employees.

Download Form I-9: https://www.uscis.gov/i-9

Legal Requirement: Section 1 must be completed on or before your first day of work. Section 2 must be completed within 3 business days of your start date.

Section 1: Employee Information (You Complete)

  • Full legal name
  • Other last names used
  • Address
  • Date of birth
  • Social Security Number (required for E-Verify employers)
  • Citizenship/immigration status
  • Signature and date

Section 2: Employer Verification (HR Completes)

You must present original, unexpired documents to verify your identity and work authorization. Documents must be presented in person to an HR representative.

Acceptable Documents

List A (Identity AND Work Authorization)
One document required:
  • U.S. Passport or Passport Card
  • Permanent Resident Card (Green Card)
  • Employment Authorization Document
  • Foreign passport with I-551 stamp
List B (Identity) + List C (Work Authorization)
One from each list:

List B: Driver's license, State ID, School ID, Military card

List C: Social Security card (unrestricted), U.S. birth certificate

Most Common Combinations

  • U.S. Citizens: U.S. Passport (List A) OR Driver's License + Social Security Card (List B + C)
  • Permanent Residents: Green Card (List A) OR Driver's License + Green Card (List B + C)
