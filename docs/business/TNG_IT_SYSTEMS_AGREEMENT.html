INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY SYSTEMS AND ACCEPTABLE USE AGREEMENT
This Information Technology Systems and Acceptable Use Agreement ("Agreement") governs Employee's access to and use of information technology systems, networks, and resources belonging to The NICEHR Group, LLC ("Company") and its clients during Employee's employment. By signing this Agreement, Employee acknowledges understanding of and agreement to comply with all terms herein.

1. SCOPE AND APPLICABILITY

1.1 This Agreement applies to all employees, contractors, and authorized users who access any of the following:
  • Company-owned or leased computers, laptops, tablets, mobile devices, and equipment;
  • Company email systems, messaging platforms, and communication tools;
  • Client systems, networks, and applications, including but not limited to Epic, Cerner, Meditech, Allscripts, and other EHR/EMR systems;
  • Company and client databases, data repositories, and file storage systems;
  • Internet access provided through Company or client networks;
  • Virtual Private Network (VPN) connections and remote access tools;
  • Cloud services, collaboration platforms, and software-as-a-service applications; and
  • Any other technology resources provided for business purposes.

2. AUTHORIZED USE

2.1 Business Purpose. IT systems are provided exclusively for legitimate business purposes related to Employee's job duties. Limited personal use may be permitted provided it does not:
  1. Interfere with work responsibilities or productivity;
  2. Violate any Company or client policies;
  3. Consume excessive system resources or bandwidth;
  4. Create security vulnerabilities or legal liability;
  5. Result in additional cost to Company or clients; or
  6. Expose confidential information or PHI to risk.
2.2 Access Credentials. Employee will be assigned unique login credentials for accessing systems. Employee is solely responsible for:
  1. Maintaining the confidentiality of all credentials, passwords, and access codes;
  2. Never sharing credentials with any other person, including coworkers, supervisors, or IT staff;
  3. Using strong passwords meeting minimum requirements (12+ characters, mixed case, numbers, symbols);
  4. Changing passwords as required by policy and immediately upon suspected compromise;
  5. Using multi-factor authentication where available and required;
  6. Logging off or locking sessions when stepping away from workstations; and
  7. Reporting any suspected credential compromise immediately to IT Security.
2.3 Minimum Necessary Access. Employee shall access only the data, systems, and information necessary for assigned job duties. Accessing information beyond the scope of work, including patient records not directly relevant to assigned tasks, is strictly prohibited.

3. PROHIBITED ACTIVITIES

The following activities are strictly prohibited and may result in immediate termination and legal action:
3.1 Unauthorized Access:
  • Accessing systems, accounts, or data without explicit authorization;
  • Using another person's credentials or allowing others to use Employee's credentials;
  • Attempting to bypass, circumvent, or defeat security controls or access restrictions;
  • Attempting to gain elevated privileges or administrator access without authorization;
  • Accessing patient records for personal curiosity, family members, celebrities, or any unauthorized purpose;
  • Accessing systems outside of assigned work hours without prior approval;
  • Using credentials or access that should have been revoked or expired.
3.2 Data Misuse:
  • Copying, downloading, exporting, or printing data without authorization;
  • Transmitting confidential data or PHI via unsecured methods (personal email, text, unauthorized cloud storage);
  • Taking screenshots, photographs, or recordings of PHI or confidential information;
  • Storing confidential data or PHI on personal devices, USB drives, or personal cloud storage;
  • Emailing confidential information to personal email accounts;
  • Sharing data with unauthorized individuals, including family members or friends;
  • Retaining copies of data after the legitimate business need has ended.
3.3 Harmful Activities:
  • Introducing malware, viruses, ransomware, or any malicious code;
  • Attempting to damage, disrupt, overload, or interfere with systems or networks;
  • Port scanning, network sniffing, packet capture, or security testing without written authorization;
  • Disabling, circumventing, or interfering with antivirus, firewalls, or security software;
  • Using systems for cryptocurrency mining, distributed computing, or other resource-intensive unauthorized activities;
  • Intercepting or monitoring network traffic or communications of others.
3.4 Inappropriate Content and Behavior:
  • Accessing, storing, transmitting, or displaying pornographic, obscene, sexually explicit, or offensive material;
  • Engaging in harassment, discrimination, bullying, or threats via electronic communications;
  • Using systems for gambling, gaming, or streaming entertainment during work hours;
  • Conducting personal business activities or outside employment using Company resources;
  • Engaging in any illegal activities whatsoever;
  • Sending chain letters, spam, or mass unsolicited communications;
  • Posting defamatory, libelous, or false statements about Company, clients, or individuals.
3.5 Unauthorized Software and Modifications:
  • Installing unauthorized software, applications, or browser extensions;
  • Downloading software, tools, or files from untrusted or unknown sources;
  • Using peer-to-peer file sharing applications;
  • Modifying system configurations, settings, or registry entries without authorization;
  • Disabling automatic updates or security patches;
  • Installing personal software using business licenses;
  • Removing or altering Company-installed software or agents.

4. EMAIL AND ELECTRONIC COMMUNICATIONS

4.1 Professional Standards. All electronic communications, including email, instant messaging, and collaboration tools, must be professional, courteous, and appropriate for a business environment. Communications represent Company and should reflect positively on the organization.
4.2 Confidential Information. Do not send confidential information, including PHI, via unencrypted email or unsecured methods. Use only Company-approved secure communication methods for transmitting sensitive data. When in doubt, do not send.
4.3 Phishing and Social Engineering. Be vigilant for phishing attempts and social engineering attacks:
  • Do not click on suspicious links or open unexpected attachments;
  • Verify requests for sensitive information through a separate communication channel;
  • Never provide credentials in response to email requests;
  • Report suspicious emails immediately to IT Security;
  • Do not forward suspicious emails to others.
4.4 Retention and Legal Holds. Business communications are subject to retention requirements and may be subject to legal holds. Do not delete emails or communications that may be relevant to pending or anticipated legal matters, audits, or investigations.

5. PHYSICAL SECURITY

5.1 Employee shall maintain physical security of all devices and materials:
  • Never leave devices unattended in public places, vehicles, or unsecured areas;
  • Lock workstations immediately when stepping away (Windows: Win+L, Mac: Ctrl+Cmd+Q);
  • Use cable locks to secure laptops in shared workspaces or hotel rooms;
  • Report lost or stolen devices immediately to Company (within 1 hour of discovery);
  • Do not allow unauthorized persons to use Company or client devices;
  • Secure all printed materials containing confidential information;
  • Use privacy screens in public areas;
  • Shred confidential documents when no longer needed;
  • Secure badges and access cards; report lost badges immediately.

6. REMOTE ACCESS AND MOBILE DEVICES

6.1 VPN Requirements. Use only Company-approved VPN connections for remote access to Company and client systems. Do not use personal VPN services or proxy servers to access business systems.
6.2 Network Security. Avoid using public Wi-Fi (airports, hotels, coffee shops) for accessing confidential systems. If public Wi-Fi must be used, always connect via VPN before accessing any business systems. Never access banking, healthcare, or other sensitive systems on public networks without VPN.
6.3 Personal Devices (BYOD). If using personal devices for work purposes, Employee must:
  • Maintain current operating system and security updates at all times;
  • Enable full-device encryption;
  • Use strong passcodes (minimum 6 digits or complex password);
  • Enable automatic screen lock (maximum 5-minute timeout);
  • Install required Mobile Device Management (MDM) software if required;
  • Consent to remote wipe capability for Company data;
  • Not jailbreak or root devices;
  • Not store PHI or highly confidential data on personal devices; and
  • Notify Company before selling, trading, or disposing of devices used for work.

7. CLIENT SYSTEM ACCESS

IMPORTANT: When accessing client systems (hospitals, health systems, clinics), Employee must comply with all client IT policies in addition to this Agreement. Client policies may be more restrictive than Company policies. In case of conflict, the more restrictive policy applies.
7.1 Employee agrees to:
  • Access only systems and applications required for assigned work;
  • Complete all required client security training, background checks, and credentialing before accessing systems;
  • Follow client badge, authentication, and physical access procedures;
  • Respect all client-designated restricted areas;
  • Not install any software on client systems without explicit authorization;
  • Not retain any client data, including test data, after the engagement ends;
  • Report security incidents to both the client and Company immediately;
  • Comply with client-specific policies regarding USB devices, personal devices, and removable media; and
  • Sign any additional confidentiality or access agreements required by clients.

8. INCIDENT REPORTING

8.1 Employee must immediately report any of the following to supervisor and IT Security:
  • Suspected or confirmed security breaches or unauthorized access;
  • Lost or stolen devices, badges, or access credentials;
  • Unauthorized access to systems or data by Employee or others;
  • Receipt of phishing emails or social engineering attempts;
  • Malware infections, ransomware, or suspicious system behavior;
  • Discovery of vulnerabilities or security weaknesses;
  • Violations of this policy by Employee or others;
  • Any potential or actual breach of PHI or confidential data.
8.2 Reporting Contact: Email security@nicehrgroup.com or call the IT Security Hotline. When in doubt, report it.

9. MONITORING AND NO EXPECTATION OF PRIVACY

9.1 EMPLOYEE HAS NO EXPECTATION OF PRIVACY when using Company or client IT systems. Company and its clients reserve the right to:
  • Monitor, access, review, copy, and record all system activity without notice;
  • Inspect all files, emails, messages, and data stored on or transmitted through systems;
  • Monitor network traffic, internet usage, and application usage;
  • Access devices remotely for security, maintenance, or administrative purposes;
  • Retain and review any data created, transmitted, or stored on systems;
  • Use monitoring tools, keyloggers, screen capture, and other surveillance technologies;
  • Review activity logs, audit trails, and access records.
9.2 Monitoring may occur at any time, without notice, for any lawful purpose, including investigating policy violations, security incidents, performance issues, or legal matters.

10. TERMINATION OF ACCESS

Upon termination of employment or end of engagement:
  • All access credentials will be immediately revoked;
  • Employee must return all Company and client equipment, badges, access cards, and materials;
  • Employee must delete all Company and client data from personal devices and accounts;
  • Employee may not retain any copies of confidential information;
  • Employee must provide all passwords, encryption keys, and access information upon request;
  • Failure to comply may result in legal action and withholding of final compensation to the extent permitted by law.

11. CONSEQUENCES OF VIOLATION

Violation of this Agreement may result in:
  • Immediate termination of employment without notice;
  • Immediate revocation of all system access;
  • Civil liability for damages, including liquidated damages;
  • Criminal prosecution under applicable federal and state laws;
  • Reporting to professional licensing boards;
  • Notification to affected clients;
  • Reporting to law enforcement agencies; and
  • HIPAA penalties if PHI is involved.
