EMPLOYMENT AGREEMENT

This Employment Agreement (this "Agreement") is made and entered into as of (the "Effective Date"), by and between:

THE NICEHR GROUP, LLC, a Texas limited liability company, with its principal place of business at (hereinafter referred to as the "Company" or "Employer"),

and

, an individual residing at (hereinafter referred to as "Employee").

Company and Employee are sometimes referred to herein individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Company is engaged in the business of providing healthcare information technology consulting services, including but not limited to Epic electronic health record implementation, optimization, training, and support services;

WHEREAS, Company desires to employ Employee, and Employee desires to be employed by Company, upon the terms and conditions set forth herein;

WHEREAS, Employee possesses specialized skills, knowledge, and experience that Company desires to utilize in furtherance of its business;

WHEREAS, in connection with Employee's employment, Employee will have access to Company's and its clients' confidential and proprietary information, trade secrets, and Protected Health Information;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

ARTICLE 1: DEFINITIONS

1.1 "Affiliate" means any entity that directly or indirectly controls, is controlled by, or is under common control with a Party.

1.2 "Client" means any healthcare organization, hospital, physician practice, or other entity to which Company provides services or has provided services during the Term.

1.3 "Confidential Information" has the meaning set forth in Article 7.

1.4 "Intellectual Property" means all patents, copyrights, trademarks, trade secrets, know-how, inventions, discoveries, improvements, works of authorship, software, documentation, and other intellectual property rights.

1.5 "Protected Health Information" or "PHI" has the meaning set forth in 45 C.F.R. § 160.103.

1.6 "Term" means the period of Employee's employment under this Agreement.

1.7 "Work Product" has the meaning set forth in Section 8.1.

2.2 Duties and Responsibilities. Employee shall perform all duties and responsibilities associated with Employee's position as set forth in Exhibit A (Job Description), attached hereto and incorporated herein by reference, together with such other duties as may be reasonably assigned by Company. Employee's duties may include, but are not limited to:

  4. Preparing documentation, reports, and deliverables as required;
  5. Maintaining required certifications and completing continuing education;
  6. Complying with all Client policies, procedures, and credentialing requirements;
  7. Such other duties as may be assigned consistent with Employee's position.

2.3 Best Efforts; Full-Time Commitment. Employee agrees to devote Employee's full professional time, attention, skill, and best efforts to the performance of Employee's duties hereunder. During the Term, Employee shall not, without the prior written consent of Company:

  3. Have any ownership interest in any business that competes with Company, except for passive investments of less than five percent (5%) in publicly traded companies.

  4. Employee is willing and able to travel as required by Company's business needs.

  1. Professional conduct and ethics;

  3. Travel and expense reimbursement;
  4. Timekeeping and attendance;
  5. Use of Company and Client information technology systems;
  6. HIPAA privacy and security requirements;
  7. Anti-harassment and non-discrimination.

ARTICLE 5: TERM AND TERMINATION

5.1 Start Date. Employee's employment shall commence on:

(the "Start Date")

5.2 Term. This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue until terminated by either Party in accordance with this Article 5.

5.3 Termination by Company Without Cause. Company may terminate Employee's employment at any time, without cause, upon providing Employee with:

  1. Written notice of termination; and
  2. Payment of all accrued but unpaid wages, unused PTO (if applicable under Company policy), and expense reimbursements through the date of termination.

5.4 Termination by Company for Cause. Company may terminate Employee's employment immediately for "Cause," which shall include, but not be limited to:

  1. Material breach of this Agreement or any Company policy;
  2. Failure or refusal to perform assigned duties;
  3. Insubordination or refusal to follow lawful directives;
  4. Dishonesty, fraud, theft, or misappropriation of Company or Client property;
  5. Conviction of, or plea of guilty or no contest to, any felony or any crime involving moral turpitude;
  6. Violation of HIPAA or any law, regulation, or Client requirement regarding patient privacy or data security;
  7. Failure to maintain required certifications, licenses, or credentials;
  8. Harassment, discrimination, or violence in the workplace;
  9. Unauthorized disclosure of Confidential Information;
  10. Gross negligence or willful misconduct;
  11. Failed drug or alcohol test, or working while impaired;
  12. Any conduct that, in Company's reasonable judgment, is harmful to Company's reputation or business interests.

5.5 Termination by Employee. Employee may terminate employment at any time by providing Company with at least two (2) weeks' advance written notice. Company may, in its discretion, waive the notice period and accelerate the termination date.

5.6 Obligations Upon Termination. Upon termination of employment for any reason, Employee shall:

  1. Immediately return to Company all Company property, including but not limited to: laptop computers, mobile devices, access badges, keys, credit cards, documents, files, and any materials containing Confidential Information;
  2. Return or destroy all copies of Confidential Information in Employee's possession or control;
  3. Complete and submit all outstanding timesheets and expense reports;
  4. Cooperate with Company to ensure orderly transition of responsibilities;
  5. Provide all passwords, access credentials, and other information necessary for Company to access systems and accounts;
  6. Comply with all post-employment obligations under this Agreement.

5.7 Final Pay. Upon termination, Company shall pay Employee all earned but unpaid wages and unused accrued PTO (if applicable) in accordance with applicable law.

5.8 Survival. Articles 7 (Confidentiality), 8 (Intellectual Property), 9 (Non-Solicitation), 10 (Compliance), and 12 (Dispute Resolution) shall survive termination of this Agreement.

ARTICLE 6: CLIENT REQUIREMENTS AND CREDENTIALING

6.1 Client Requirements. Employee acknowledges that Company's Clients may impose requirements as a condition of Employee's access to Client facilities and systems, including but not limited to:

  1. Criminal background checks;
  2. Drug and alcohol screening;
  3. Immunization and health screening requirements;
  4. Professional reference verification;
  5. Verification of education, certifications, and employment history;
  6. Completion of Client-specific training and orientation;
  7. Execution of Client confidentiality agreements.

6.2 Cooperation. Employee agrees to cooperate fully with all credentialing requirements and to provide accurate and complete information. Employee understands that failure to satisfy Client requirements may result in Employee's inability to work on certain assignments and may constitute grounds for termination.

6.3 Disclosure. Employee agrees to promptly disclose to Company any arrest, indictment, conviction, or other matter that may affect Employee's ability to satisfy Client requirements or maintain required credentials.

6.4 Certifications. Employee shall maintain all professional certifications required for Employee's position. Company may, in its discretion, provide financial assistance for certification examinations and continuing education, subject to Company policy.

ARTICLE 7: CONFIDENTIALITY

7.1 Definition of Confidential Information. "Confidential Information" means all non-public information, in any form, relating to Company, its Affiliates, or its Clients, including but not limited to:

  1. Business Information: Business plans, strategies, projections, financial information, pricing, proposals, contracts, client lists, vendor relationships, and marketing plans;
  2. Technical Information: Software, systems, methodologies, processes, configurations, documentation, training materials, and know-how;
  3. Client Information: Client identities, contracts, project details, system configurations, workflows, and any information obtained while performing services for Clients;
  4. Protected Health Information: Any information relating to the past, present, or future physical or mental health condition of an individual, the provision of healthcare, or payment for healthcare that identifies the individual;
  5. Personnel Information: Employee lists, compensation data, performance evaluations, and other human resources information;
  6. Trade Secrets: Any information that derives independent economic value from not being generally known and is subject to reasonable efforts to maintain its secrecy.

7.2 Obligations. Employee agrees to:

  1. Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence;
  2. Not use Confidential Information for any purpose other than performing Employee's duties;
  3. Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written authorization;
  4. Take all reasonable precautions to prevent unauthorized disclosure;
  5. Immediately notify Company of any actual or suspected unauthorized disclosure;
  6. Return or destroy all Confidential Information upon termination or upon Company's request.

7.3 Exceptions. Confidential Information does not include information that:

  1. Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of Employee;
  2. Was known to Employee prior to disclosure by Company, as documented by written records;
  3. Is rightfully received from a third party without restriction;
  4. Is independently developed by Employee without use of Confidential Information.

7.4 Required Disclosures. If Employee is compelled by law, court order, or governmental authority to disclose Confidential Information, Employee shall:

  1. Provide Company with prompt written notice to allow Company to seek protective relief;
  2. Cooperate with Company in seeking such protective relief;
  3. Disclose only such information as is legally required.

7.5 Duration. Employee's obligations under this Article 7 shall:

  1. With respect to trade secrets, continue indefinitely for so long as such information constitutes a trade secret under applicable law;
  2. With respect to other Confidential Information, continue for a period of five (5) years following termination of employment;
  3. With respect to PHI, continue indefinitely in accordance with HIPAA requirements.

7.6 Separate NDA. Employee acknowledges that Employee has executed or will execute a separate Non-Disclosure Agreement, the terms of which are incorporated herein by reference. In the event of any conflict, the more restrictive provision shall control.

ARTICLE 8: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

8.1 Work Product Defined. "Work Product" means all inventions, discoveries, improvements, ideas, concepts, designs, works of authorship, software, documentation, training materials, processes, techniques, and other intellectual property, whether or not patentable or copyrightable, that Employee conceives, develops, creates, or reduces to practice, alone or jointly with others, during the Term and that:

  1. Result from Employee's work for Company;
  2. Relate to Company's or any Client's business or actual or demonstrably anticipated research or development;
  3. Are developed using Company's or any Client's equipment, supplies, facilities, or Confidential Information.

8.2 Assignment. Employee hereby irrevocably assigns and transfers to Company all right, title, and interest in and to all Work Product, including all Intellectual Property rights therein. Employee agrees that all Work Product shall be considered "works made for hire" under the Copyright Act to the extent applicable.

8.3 Further Assurances. Employee agrees to:

  1. Promptly disclose all Work Product to Company;
  2. Execute all documents and take all actions necessary to perfect Company's ownership;
  3. Assist Company in obtaining and enforcing patents, copyrights, and other protections;
  4. Maintain adequate records of Work Product development.

8.4 Moral Rights. To the extent permitted by law, Employee waives any moral rights or rights of attribution in any Work Product.

8.5 Prior Inventions. Employee has listed on Exhibit B all inventions, if any, that Employee made prior to employment that Employee wishes to exclude from this Agreement. If no such list is attached, Employee represents that there are no such prior inventions.

8.6 Client Ownership. Employee acknowledges that Work Product created for Clients may be owned by such Clients pursuant to Company's agreements with Clients, and Employee hereby assigns all such Work Product to Company for further assignment to Clients as required.

ARTICLE 9: NON-SOLICITATION

9.1 Non-Solicitation of Clients. During employment and for a period of twelve (12) months following termination for any reason, Employee shall not, directly or indirectly:

  1. Solicit, divert, or accept business from any Client for whom Employee performed services or about whom Employee received Confidential Information during the twenty-four (24) months preceding termination;
  2. Encourage, induce, or attempt to induce any Client to reduce or terminate its business relationship with Company;
  3. Provide services to any Client that are competitive with services provided by Company.

9.2 Non-Solicitation of Employees. During employment and for a period of twelve (12) months following termination for any reason, Employee shall not, directly or indirectly:

  1. Solicit, recruit, hire, or attempt to hire any employee or consultant of Company;
  2. Encourage, induce, or attempt to induce any employee or consultant to terminate their relationship with Company;
  3. Provide information about Company employees to any recruiter, competitor, or other third party for recruitment purposes.

9.3 Reasonableness. Employee acknowledges that the restrictions in this Article 9 are reasonable and necessary to protect Company's legitimate business interests, including its client relationships and workforce stability.

9.4 Blue Pencil. If any provision of this Article 9 is found to be unenforceable, the court shall modify such provision to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable, and the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

ARTICLE 10: COMPLIANCE

10.1 HIPAA Compliance. Employee agrees to:

  1. Complete all required HIPAA training within the timeframes specified by Company;
  2. Comply with all HIPAA Privacy Rule and Security Rule requirements;
  3. Access, use, and disclose PHI only as necessary to perform assigned duties and in accordance with the minimum necessary standard;
  4. Report any actual or suspected breach of PHI immediately to Company;
  5. Never access PHI for personal reasons or out of curiosity;
  6. Comply with all Client policies regarding PHI access and handling.

10.2 Legal Compliance. Employee agrees to comply with all applicable federal, state, and local laws, regulations, and ordinances in the performance of Employee's duties.

10.3 Professional Standards. Employee agrees to maintain the highest standards of professional conduct and ethics and to comply with all applicable professional codes of conduct.

10.4 Reporting Obligations. Employee agrees to promptly report to Company any:

  1. Violation of law, regulation, or Company policy;
  2. Breach or suspected breach of Confidential Information or PHI;
  3. Harassment, discrimination, or other workplace misconduct;
  4. Safety hazards or concerns;
  5. Conflicts of interest.

ARTICLE 11: REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

11.1 Employee Representations. Employee represents and warrants that:

  1. Employee has the legal right to work in the United States and will provide documentation as required by law;
  2. All information provided in Employee's employment application and during the hiring process is true, accurate, and complete;
  3. Employee is not bound by any agreement that would prevent Employee from performing Employee's duties;
  4. Employee possesses all certifications, licenses, and credentials represented in Employee's application;
  5. Employee has disclosed all potential conflicts of interest;
  6. Employee has no medical condition that would prevent Employee from performing the essential functions of the position, with or without reasonable accommodation.

11.2 Consequences of Misrepresentation. Any material misrepresentation by Employee shall constitute grounds for immediate termination and may result in forfeiture of any unpaid compensation or benefits.

ARTICLE 12: DISPUTE RESOLUTION

12.1 Informal Resolution. The Parties agree to attempt in good faith to resolve any dispute arising under this Agreement through informal negotiation.

12.2 Binding Arbitration. Any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or relating to this Agreement, or the breach, termination, or validity thereof, that cannot be resolved through informal negotiation shall be resolved by binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association (AAA) in accordance with its Employment Arbitration Rules.

12.3 Arbitration Terms.

  1. The arbitration shall be conducted by a single arbitrator;
  2. The arbitration shall take place in , Texas, or another mutually agreed location;
  3. The arbitrator shall apply the substantive law of Texas;
  4. The arbitrator's decision shall be final and binding, and judgment may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction;
  5. Each Party shall bear its own costs and attorneys' fees, except as otherwise provided by law.

12.4 Injunctive Relief. Notwithstanding the foregoing, Company may seek injunctive or other equitable relief in any court of competent jurisdiction to enforce the provisions of Articles 7, 8, and 9, as such violations would cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be inadequate.

12.5 Class Action Waiver. To the fullest extent permitted by law, Employee waives any right to bring, join, or participate in any class action, collective action, or representative action against Company.

ARTICLE 13: GENERAL PROVISIONS

13.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement, together with the Exhibits attached hereto, the Employee Handbook, and any separate NDA or confidentiality agreement, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous agreements, representations, and understandings.

13.2 Amendment. This Agreement may be amended or modified only by a written instrument signed by both Parties.

13.3 Waiver. No waiver of any provision of this Agreement shall be effective unless in writing and signed by the waiving Party. No failure or delay in exercising any right shall operate as a waiver thereof.

13.4 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect. The invalid or unenforceable provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid and enforceable.

13.5 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Texas, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

13.6 Notices. All notices required or permitted under this Agreement shall be in writing and shall be deemed delivered when:

  1. Delivered personally;
  2. Sent by overnight courier (delivery confirmed);
  3. Sent by certified or registered mail, return receipt requested; or
  4. Sent by email (with confirmation of receipt).

Notices shall be sent to the addresses set forth on the signature page or as updated by written notice.

13.7 Assignment. Employee may not assign or transfer this Agreement or any rights or obligations hereunder. Company may assign this Agreement to any successor or affiliate without Employee's consent.

13.8 Counterparts. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument. Electronic signatures shall be deemed valid and binding.

13.9 Headings. Section headings are for convenience only and shall not affect the interpretation of this Agreement.

13.10 Third-Party Beneficiaries. Except as expressly provided herein, nothing in this Agreement shall confer any rights upon any person or entity other than the Parties and their respective successors and permitted assigns.

13.11 Construction. This Agreement shall be construed without regard to any presumption against the Party that drafted it.
