4. TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 Start Date. Employee's employment shall commence on:

4.2 Term. This Agreement shall remain in effect until terminated by either party in accordance with this Section.

5. CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 Confidential Information. Employee acknowledges that during employment, Employee will have access to confidential and proprietary information belonging to the Company and its clients, including but not limited to: business plans, client lists, pricing information, technical data, trade secrets, patient health information (PHI), and other non-public information ("Confidential Information").

5.2 Non-Disclosure. Employee agrees to:

  1. Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence;
  2. Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent;
  3. Use Confidential Information only for the purpose of performing job duties;
  4. Return all Confidential Information upon termination.

5.3 Survival. The confidentiality obligations shall survive termination of employment indefinitely with respect to trade secrets and for a period of three (3) years for other Confidential Information.

6. NON-SOLICITATION

6.1 Non-Solicitation of Clients. During employment and for a period of twelve (12) months following termination, Employee shall not directly or indirectly solicit, divert, or take away any client of the Company with whom Employee had contact during employment.

6.2 Non-Solicitation of Employees. During employment and for a period of twelve (12) months following termination, Employee shall not directly or indirectly solicit, recruit, or hire any employee or consultant of the Company.

7.1 Work Product. All work product, inventions, discoveries, improvements, and developments created by Employee in the course of employment shall be the sole and exclusive property of the Company or its clients, as applicable.

7.2 Assignment. Employee hereby assigns to the Company all right, title, and interest in any work product created during employment.

8.1 HIPAA. Employee agrees to comply with all requirements of the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA), including completion of required training and adherence to policies regarding Protected Health Information (PHI).

8.2 Policies. Employee agrees to comply with all Company policies and procedures, including but not limited to the Employee Handbook, IT Acceptable Use Policy, and Travel Policy.

8.3 Client Requirements. Employee agrees to comply with all client facility requirements, including credentialing, background checks, drug screening, and immunization requirements.
