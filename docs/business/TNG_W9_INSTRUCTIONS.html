W-9 FORM INSTRUCTIONS
DEADLINE: A completed W-9 form must be submitted before your first payment can be processed. Submit within 3 business days of your start date.

What is Form W-9?

Form W-9 (Request for Taxpayer Identification Number and Certification) is an IRS form used to provide your correct Taxpayer Identification Number (TIN) to The NICEHR Group, LLC. We are required by law to collect this information to report payments made to you to the IRS.

Download Form W-9: https://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-pdf/fw9.pdf

Who Must Complete a W-9?

Worker Classification Tax Form Required Year-End Form Received
W-2 Employee W-4 (not W-9) Form W-2
1099 Independent Contractor W-9 Form 1099-NEC

If you are classified as a 1099 Independent Contractor, you must complete and submit Form W-9. If you are a W-2 Employee, complete Form W-4 instead (see Tax Forms Instructions document).

Step-by-Step Instructions

Line 1: Name
  • Enter your legal name exactly as shown on your tax return
  • For individuals: First Name, Middle Initial, Last Name
  • For LLCs/businesses: Enter the business name
Line 2: Business Name (if different)
  • If you operate under a business name, trade name, or DBA, enter it here
  • Leave blank if same as Line 1 or not applicable
Line 3: Federal Tax Classification
  • Individual/sole proprietor: Check this if you are self-employed
  • Single-member LLC: Check "Individual" (disregarded entity)
  • C Corporation / S Corporation: Check appropriate box if incorporated
  • Partnership: Check if operating as a partnership
  • LLC (C Corp or S Corp): Check LLC and indicate tax classification
Line 4: Exemptions (if applicable)
  • Most individuals leave this blank
  • Exemption codes apply only to certain entities (corporations, tax-exempt organizations)
Lines 5-6: Address
  • Enter your current mailing address
  • This address will be used for 1099 form mailing
Line 7: Account Numbers (Optional)
  • Leave blank - not required for our purposes
Part I: Taxpayer Identification Number (TIN)
  • Individuals: Enter your Social Security Number (SSN)
  • Businesses: Enter your Employer Identification Number (EIN)
  • Single-member LLCs typically use the owner's SSN
Part II: Certification
  • Read the certification statements
  • Sign and date the form
  • The form is not valid without your signature

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Forgetting to sign and date the form
  • Using a nickname instead of legal name
  • Incorrect or missing TIN (SSN or EIN)
  • Checking multiple boxes on Line 3
  • Using an outdated version of the form

How to Submit

  • Email: HR@nicehrgroup.com (PDF attachment)
  • In Person: Present to HR representative

Important: Your W-9 contains sensitive information (SSN/EIN). Do not send via unsecured email. Use encrypted email or deliver in person when possible.
