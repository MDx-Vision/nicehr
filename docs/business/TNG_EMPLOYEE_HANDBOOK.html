1. WELCOME AND INTRODUCTION

Welcome to The NICEHR Group, LLC ("Company"). We are a healthcare IT consulting firm specializing in Electronic Health Record (EHR) implementations, Technical Dress Rehearsals (TDR), go-live support, and technical services for healthcare organizations nationwide.

This Employee Handbook ("Handbook") establishes the policies, procedures, and expectations that govern your employment. This Handbook is not a contract of employment and does not create any contractual rights. The Company reserves the right to modify, revoke, suspend, or add to any policy at any time, with or without notice.

You are responsible for reading, understanding, and complying with all policies contained herein. If you have questions about any policy, please contact your supervisor or Human Resources.

2. EMPLOYMENT RELATIONSHIP

2.1 At-Will Employment

Your employment with the Company is "at-will." This means that either you or the Company may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause, and with or without notice. No supervisor, manager, or representative of the Company has the authority to enter into any agreement for employment for any specified period of time or to make any agreement contrary to the at-will nature of your employment, unless such agreement is in writing and signed by an authorized officer of the Company.

2.2 Employment Classifications

Classification Description Overtime
W-2 Non-Exempt (Hourly) Employees paid hourly based on time worked Eligible (1.5× for hours over 40/week)
W-2 Exempt (Salaried) Employees meeting FLSA exemption criteria Not eligible
1099 Independent Contractor Governed by separate contractor agreement N/A

2.3 Project-Based Employment

Employment is typically tied to specific client engagements. When an engagement ends, employment may be terminated unless reassignment to another project is available and mutually agreed upon. There is no guarantee of continued employment between projects.

3. EQUAL EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITY

The NICEHR Group is an equal opportunity employer. We prohibit discrimination and harassment based on race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity or expression, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, veteran status, marital status, pregnancy, or any other characteristic protected by applicable federal, state, or local law.

This policy applies to all terms and conditions of employment, including but not limited to recruitment, hiring, placement, promotion, termination, layoff, recall, transfer, leaves of absence, compensation, benefits, and training.

Employees who believe they have been subjected to discrimination or harassment should immediately report the conduct to their supervisor, Human Resources, or any member of management. All complaints will be investigated promptly, thoroughly, and confidentially to the extent possible. Retaliation against employees who report concerns in good faith is strictly prohibited.

4. COMPENSATION AND PAYROLL

4.1 Pay Schedule

Employees are paid bi-weekly on Fridays for the two-week period ending the previous Saturday. If a payday falls on a holiday, payment will be made on the preceding business day.

4.2 Overtime

Non-exempt employees will be paid one and one-half times (1.5×) their regular hourly rate for all hours worked in excess of forty (40) hours in a workweek. All overtime must be pre-approved by your supervisor or project manager. Working unauthorized overtime may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

4.3 Per Diem Rates

Per diem is provided to offset costs associated with working away from home. Rates are based on distance from employee's home to the work site:

Tier Distance from Home Daily Rate Conditions
Tier 1 (Remote/Travel) More than 50 miles $240.00 Employee books own accommodations
Tier 2 (Regional) 11-50 miles $175.00 Minimum 3 hours worked
Tier 3 (Local) Less than 11 miles $75.00 Standard work day
Tier 4 (Training/Orientation) Any distance $50.00 Training periods only

Per diem is paid for actual days worked and is subject to verification. Misrepresentation of per diem eligibility is grounds for termination.

4.4 Direct Deposit

All employees must enroll in direct deposit. A completed Direct Deposit Authorization Form with a voided check or bank verification letter is required prior to processing the first payroll. Changes to direct deposit information require 5 business days notice.

4.5 Tax Withholding

Employees must complete Form W-4 (Employee's Withholding Certificate) and applicable state tax forms upon hire. You are responsible for ensuring your withholding information is accurate and up to date. Changes to W-4 should be submitted to HR and take effect on the next available payroll.

5. WORK HOURS AND ATTENDANCE

5.1 Standard Work Week

The standard work week is forty (40) hours, typically Monday through Friday. However, actual schedules are determined by client requirements and may include early mornings, evenings, weekends, or holidays, particularly during go-live support periods.

5.2 Attendance and Punctuality

Regular attendance and punctuality are essential to successful project delivery. You must report to work as scheduled and remain at work for your entire shift. If you cannot report to work or will be late, you must notify your supervisor as soon as possible, and no later than one (1) hour before your scheduled start time.

5.3 No-Call, No-Show

Failure to report to work without proper notification for two (2) consecutive scheduled workdays will be considered job abandonment and voluntary resignation. The Company will process your separation accordingly.

6. TIME REPORTING AND TIMESHEETS

6.1 Timesheet Requirements

All employees must accurately record their time worked daily using the Company's designated timekeeping system. Timesheets must include:

  • Date of work
  • Start and end times
  • Total hours worked (regular and overtime separately)
  • Project/client assignment codes
  • Detailed task descriptions sufficient to verify work performed
  • Break time deductions where applicable

6.2 Submission Deadline

DEADLINE: Timesheets must be submitted by Monday at 12:00 PM Eastern Time for the preceding work week. Late timesheets may delay payment.

Repeated late submissions will result in disciplinary action.

6.3 Time Falsification

Falsifying time records is a serious offense and grounds for immediate termination. This includes:

  • Recording time not actually worked
  • Altering, tampering with, or falsifying time records
  • Having another person record time on your behalf
  • Failing to record all time worked

8. CONFIDENTIALITY AND DATA PROTECTION

8.1 Confidential Information

During your employment, you will have access to confidential and proprietary information belonging to the Company, its clients, and affiliates. Confidential Information includes, but is not limited to:

  • Client systems, configurations, workflows, and data
  • Protected Health Information (PHI) and patient data
  • Business strategies, methodologies, and proprietary processes
  • Pricing, contracts, proposals, and financial information
  • Employee and consultant information
  • Trade secrets and intellectual property

8.2 Confidentiality Obligations

You agree to:

  • Not disclose Confidential Information to any unauthorized person or entity
  • Not use Confidential Information for personal gain or benefit of others
  • Take reasonable precautions to protect Confidential Information
  • Return all Confidential Information upon termination
  • Not retain any copies after employment ends

These obligations survive termination of employment indefinitely.

8.3 Non-Disclosure Agreement

All employees must sign a separate Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) as a condition of employment. Violation of confidentiality obligations may result in immediate termination, civil liability, and criminal prosecution.

9. HIPAA COMPLIANCE

9.1 Protected Health Information (PHI)

As a healthcare IT consulting firm, we frequently access Protected Health Information (PHI) subject to the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA). All employees must complete HIPAA training prior to accessing any client systems containing PHI.

9.2 HIPAA Requirements

You must:

  • Access only the minimum necessary PHI required for your job duties
  • Never access PHI for personal reasons, curiosity, or unauthorized purposes
  • Never share PHI with unauthorized individuals, including family and friends
  • Use only secure, approved methods for transmitting PHI
  • Never take screenshots, photographs, or recordings of PHI
  • Log off systems when not in active use
  • Report any suspected breaches immediately

9.3 Breach Reporting

IMMEDIATE REPORTING REQUIRED: Any suspected or actual breach of PHI must be reported immediately (within 24 hours) to your supervisor and the Company's Privacy Officer. Failure to report a breach is itself a violation of this policy.

9.4 HIPAA Penalties

HIPAA violations can result in severe penalties:

  • Civil penalties up to $1,500,000 per violation category per year
  • Criminal penalties including fines up to $250,000 and imprisonment up to 10 years
  • Personal liability - you may be held individually responsible
  • Immediate termination of employment
  • Exclusion from federal healthcare programs

10. PROFESSIONAL CONDUCT

10.1 Code of Conduct

All employees are expected to:

  • Act with honesty, integrity, and professionalism at all times
  • Treat colleagues, clients, patients, and visitors with respect and dignity
  • Maintain professional appearance appropriate to the client site
  • Communicate professionally in all interactions, written and verbal
  • Avoid conflicts of interest and report any potential conflicts
  • Comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and policies
  • Represent the Company positively in all professional interactions

10.2 Prohibited Conduct

The following conduct is strictly prohibited and may result in immediate termination:

  • Harassment, discrimination, bullying, or retaliation
  • Violence, threats, intimidation, or aggressive behavior
  • Theft, fraud, embezzlement, or dishonesty
  • Possession of weapons on client sites or Company premises
  • Being under the influence of drugs or alcohol at work
  • Insubordination or refusal to follow reasonable instructions
  • Falsification of records, credentials, or applications
  • Unauthorized access to systems, data, or restricted areas
  • Breach of confidentiality or HIPAA violations

10.3 Social Media Policy

  • Never post Confidential Information, client names, or patient information on social media
  • Do not represent yourself as speaking on behalf of the Company unless authorized
  • Use good judgment; posts may reflect on your professional reputation and the Company
  • Do not disparage the Company, clients, colleagues, or competitors

11. CLIENT SITE POLICIES

When working at client sites, you must comply with all client policies and procedures in addition to Company policies. These may include:

  • Badge and identification requirements
  • Visitor sign-in/sign-out procedures
  • Parking restrictions and designations
  • Dress code requirements (which may be more formal than Company policy)
  • Restricted areas and access controls
  • IT and security policies
  • Patient interaction protocols

Failure to comply with client policies may result in removal from the engagement and potential termination.

12. TECHNOLOGY AND INFORMATION SECURITY

All employees must sign and comply with the IT Systems and Acceptable Use Agreement. Key requirements include:

  • Use strong, unique passwords and never share credentials
  • Lock your workstation when stepping away
  • Do not install unauthorized software on Company or client systems
  • Report suspicious emails, security incidents, and lost devices immediately
  • Use only approved methods for file sharing and data transmission
  • Do not connect personal devices to client networks without authorization
  • Do not store Confidential Information or PHI on personal devices

You have no expectation of privacy when using Company or client technology resources. All activity may be monitored, logged, and reviewed.

13. HEALTH, SAFETY, AND CREDENTIALING

13.1 Drug-Free Workplace

The Company maintains a drug-free workplace. The use, possession, distribution, or sale of illegal drugs or controlled substances on company time, client premises, or Company property is strictly prohibited and grounds for immediate termination. Random drug testing may be conducted as permitted by law.

All employees must pass background checks and pre-employment drug screening. Healthcare client sites typically require additional credentialing including:

14. PERFORMANCE AND TERMINATION

14.1 Performance Expectations

You are expected to perform your duties competently and professionally. Performance issues may be addressed through coaching, feedback, verbal or written warnings, or termination as appropriate.

14.2 Resignation

If you choose to resign, we request a minimum of fourteen (14) calendar days written notice. Failure to provide adequate notice may result in:

  • Forfeiture of final per diem payment
  • Reduction of final pay rate to training rate
  • Notation in personnel file affecting rehire eligibility

14.3 Termination for Cause

The Company may terminate employment immediately for cause, including but not limited to:

  • Violation of Company or client policies
  • Misconduct, insubordination, or unprofessional behavior
  • Poor performance or failure to meet job requirements
  • Misrepresentation of qualifications or falsification of records
  • HIPAA or confidentiality violations
  • Failure to meet or maintain credentialing requirements
  • Abandonment (no-call, no-show for 2+ days)

14.4 Return of Property

Upon termination, you must immediately return all Company and client property, including badges, equipment, documents, access credentials, and any materials containing Confidential Information. Failure to return property may result in deductions from final pay as permitted by law and potential legal action.

15. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

15.1 Arbitration Agreement

Any dispute arising out of or related to your employment, including but not limited to claims of discrimination, harassment, wrongful termination, breach of contract, wage and hour disputes, or any other employment-related claim, shall be resolved through final and binding arbitration in accordance with the rules of the American Arbitration Association.

This agreement to arbitrate does not prevent you from filing a charge with the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC) or similar state or local agency, but any subsequent claim for damages must be arbitrated.

15.2 Waiver of Class Action

You agree to resolve all disputes on an individual basis only. You expressly waive any right to participate in any class action, collective action, or representative proceeding, whether in arbitration, court, or any other forum.

15.3 Governing Law

This Handbook and your employment relationship shall be governed by the laws of the State of New York, without regard to conflict of law principles.
